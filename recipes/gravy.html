Gravy Recipe

Description

Gravy is a type of food that is used as a sauce to add to foods that do not have the desired amount of moisture content or flavor. It is also a way to use available food stuffs to add calories and fortify meals. There may be no difference between sauce and gravy for most purposes although gravies tend to be of a heavier viscocity and are often added to the meal more liberally.

The preparation of gravy can vary widely but for the purposes of the recipe we will be combining a liquid with a gelatinizing starch and flavoring. The two common gelatinizing starches we will concern ourselves with will be flour in the form of a roux and corn starch. The liquids as well as the flavorings can vary widely and are often choosen in tandem with the meal the gravy will be served with.

Ingredients

Choose one method of gelatinizing your starch:

  • Roux
    • Flour
    • Fat
  • Corn Starch
    • Corn Starch
    • Cold Water

Choose a liquid or combination of liquids:

  • Chicken, Turkey, Vegetable or Beef Stock
  • Water and some flavoring like soy sauce
  • Milk
  • Pan drippings/fond dissolved into wine or water

Seasonings add Additions:

  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Ground or shredded meats (Sausage is popular)
  • Mushrooms
  • Cooked diced Vegetables
  • Herbs
  • Acids (Citrus or Vinegars)

Steps

If you are using a roux follow these steps:

  1. Heat your fat at 70% heat.
  2. Add your flour.
  3. Stir it around.
  4. Don't let it burn but do let it bubble and cook for a while.
  5. Add your liquid, seasonings, and add ins.
  6. Reduce heat to 50%.
  7. Whisk it around until it is smooth.
  8. Let it come up to a gentle simmer
  9. If it is too thick add more liquid and whisk it in, If it is too thin make some more roux in a side pan and whisk it in.
  10. Taste it. If you want to add in more flavorings do that.

If you are using corn starch follow these steps:

  1. Combine your corn starch, liquid, seasonings and add ins while cold.
  2. Bring it up to a boil stirring sometimes to make sure the bottom doesn't scald.
  3. If it is too thick add some more liquid , If it is too thin make a slurry of cold water and corn starch and whisk it in returning it to boil.
  4. Taste it. If you want to add in more flavorings do that.
