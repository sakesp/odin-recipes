Mashed Potatoes

Description

Mashed Potatoes are a simple food that are often served as a side dish. Just as with their deriviative un-mashed potatoes they are highly nutritious and are well tolerated by descerning palates. The one attribute some people on the more texture sensitive side may find unappealing is the mashedness of the potatoes, which the detractors may label gooey, ploppy, slimey, mushy, or even of the defacatory nature.

The keys to making good mashed potatoes are to not boil your potatoes until they fall apart and separately to manage the consistency and flavor of the final product with the use of add ins such as butter. Potato skins may be removed or left in the final product depending on preference.

Essential Ingredients

  • Potatoes

Optional Ingredients

  • Salt (recommended)
  • Butter
  • Half and Half
  • Heavy Cream
  • Milk
  • Chicken or Turkey Stock
  • Herbs
  • Cheeses

Steps

  1. Boil your potatoes
  2. Stop boiling your potatoes when they can easily be peirced by a toothpick, fork, or paring knife.
  3. Mash your potatoes. Use a potato ricer, a stand mixer, a hand mixer, a potato masher, or a heavy duty spoon. Really you can use anything that will not contaminate the purity of your potatoes and that will also prevent your hand from being burned.
  4. Add in your add ins. Stir in the dairy or liquid indgredients until the potatoes are the consistency you desire. Then stir in you flavoring ingredients until your potatoes are the flavor you desire.
  5. Eat your mashed potatoes.
