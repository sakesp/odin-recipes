Easier BBQ Beef Brisket

Description

Beef Brisket is a popular food. It is meat. Completely meat. The best brisket can be very very good. However much of the bricket you find in the wild is either too chewy or too dry. An ancient monastic tradition from the land of Texas has perfected the making of brisket in wood burning cookers. There is also a separate and even more ancient Abrahmic tradition passed down by grandmothers in which the brisket is cooked in a moist enviroment in an indoor oven.

The key to cooking good brisket is to cook it until all the connective tissue has broken down but to not cook is so harshly that the protiens synch up and leave the meat with no more moisture. This recipe has the letters BBQ in the name to signify that the meat will will be exposed to combustible carbon if the form of wood or charcoal. This recipe also contains the word "Easier" because traditional Texas BBQ requires the stoaking of a fire outside for anywhere from 10-18 hour. Many traditional BBQ pitmasters however, wrap the briskets after some amount of time to make the brisket cook faster and to prevent moisture loss. This recipe will be easier because at the point of wrapping we will take the brisket inside to the home oven and cook it low and slow until it is done.

Ingredients

  • Brisket or Part of a Brisket (Choice or Prime Grade make a noticably better product)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Other Seasonings (If you don't mind listening to internet pitmasters scold you)

Steps

  1. Cook your brisket outside in a BBQ fueled by wood or charcoal at a temp between 210-290 for at least 3 hours.
  2. When it has good color on it and you are have had enough fun managing your fire, wrap tightly in several layers of foil or butcher's paper and take in inside to a oven pre-heated to 225.
  3. Cook it until it is 195 to 205 and feels jiggly and tender.
  4. Remove it from the oven when it is done, leave it wrapped and put a few towels around it and let it sit for 1-2 hours.
  5. Cut it against the grain and eat it.
